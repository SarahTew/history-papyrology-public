Queen's Coll.

My dear Smyly,
I have looked at the
Damanthûs stele which is a
shortened copy of the hieroglyphics
of the Rosetta, with many
blunders acc. to Bouriant (whose
edition contains several misprints)
It is dated in the 23rd year of
Epiphanes, Sorpiaeu 24 = Phar-
monthi 24 of Itet there is no

doubt. My mother who knows
a fair amount of hieroglyphics has
looked at Bouriants text & the
photograph. If there is a mistake
in the name of the months it [???] is
more likely to be in the Egyptian than
in the Macedonian, which is written
out in letters Qrpiais, while the Egyptian
is written in the ordinary way
"going out month 4" i.e.
Pharmuthi.
I am sorry that we dont think
either Αχισιου or ενατ̣η possible

readings in the papyrus you saw,
while and I do not understand your
reference of the other papyrus to
Philopater Neos. It is clearly
Philometor, and the name of
the ιερεια Αρσινοης Φιλοπατρος
in both agress with the Rosetta
& Damenthûs inscriptions, showing
she was priestess fr. the 9th of Ptol V
to the 8th of Ptol VII (or VI if
we are to believe Stack).
By the way I am reviewing his
book on the Ptolemis for the Clan.


[begin page 3]


Review. May I refer to the fact
that youa re re-editing the P.P.? I
I am going to take him for treating
Revillout so seriously. For the latter
gentlmen we are preparing a
further excursus in our next volume
on the subject of dowry. I think
we can leave him, Wessely & Mitteis
on toast.
I enclose copies of the protocols
of the two Amherst papyri. If you
refer to them in print, please call them
Lord A's. We hope to publish his ppayri
in 1900.
Yours ever
B.P.Grenfell
