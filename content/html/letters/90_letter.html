Queen's Coll.
June 1

My dear Smyly,
I was away in town yesterday, but
went to the Bodleian today, the light being some
of the best. Here are the answers to your questions
(1) P.P.II vii line 7. I read εις τουτο for
εκ τουγ εις is quite certain. Παυνι in
M's copy isnt right.
line 11. the first symbol ῐ seems to represent
αρουρα, the second 5 artabae
13. I dont understand the symbol [symbol]. [cancellation?]
[symbol] is of course ιπ(παεχια) or some other
derivative of ἵππος. But here the symbol seems
to be Μ not Π with an l through the middle.
(2) RP II.viii col II verso the word before
τιραχθηουσνται might be read as επει. In the
next line Wilcken is, I think, right: it is γαρ.
